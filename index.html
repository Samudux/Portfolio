About

I didn't start in design — I started in discipline.

As a jiu-jitsu athlete and coach, I was used to structure, repetition and continuous improvement. When I created a clothing brand for the sport, I needed to design my own graphics. That was my first contact with design — and what started as necessity quickly became obsession.

I studied graphic design, then moved into UI/UX, and eventually realized that what really interested me wasn't just interface — it was how products work. How decisions get made. How structure shapes behavior. I found myself more drawn to the problem than the screen.

I'm currently studying Digital Design and User Experience, building real projects and case studies with a focus on product thinking, UX strategy and collaboration with development teams. My goal is to work on products where I can think beyond the interface — contributing to how it works, not just how it looks.

I work in short, focused cycles. AI is part of my workflow — not to replace thinking, but to reduce friction and spend more time on the decisions that actually matter.
